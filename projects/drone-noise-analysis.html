Drone Noise Analysis

Acoustics Research

April 2024

View Repository on GitHub

Context

I am part of the Duke University Bass Connections program, a collaborative research initiative involving undergraduates, graduates, and faculty. My project team, "Using Drones to Monitor the Health of Endangered Elephants in Zambia," focuses on leveraging drones for elephant body condition assessments. As the only engineering student on the team, I handle most of the technical aspects of the project.

Research Objective

To assess the impact of drone noise on wildlife, my team and I are conducting flight tests to determine the optimal altitude and speed for monitoring elephants while minimizing disturbance. The team is primarily concerned with the DJI Phantom 4 and DJI Mavic 3 Pro, as these drones are currently used in Zambia for elephant body condition monitoring.

Methodology

Flight Test Procedures

For vertical flight tests, we fly at altitudes ranging from 120m down to 10m, stopping every 5m for 30 seconds before descending at 2m/s. For horizontal flights, we operate at 15m, 30m, and 45m, with speeds of 2, 4, and 6 m/s. A microphone positioned at elephant ear height records drone noise as the UAV approaches.

Data Collection & Analysis

Preliminary analysis of our recordings in Raven Lite has allowed us to visualize noise levels across different flight parameters. The data was processed in Python utilizing microphone data to generate sound pressure level (SPL) vs frequency plots at various altitudes. This analysis helps identify the frequency characteristics of drone noise and how they vary with flight parameters.

Python Data Processing Pipeline

I developed a Python-based data processing pipeline to analyze the acoustic data. The pipeline processes raw microphone recordings, applies frequency domain analysis, and generates visualizations showing SPL vs frequency relationships at different altitudes. This allows for systematic comparison of noise characteristics across flight profiles.

Results & Outcomes

The analysis revealed significant variations in noise levels with altitude and speed. The frequency analysis identified key noise characteristics that can inform optimal flight parameters for wildlife monitoring. The data processing pipeline enables systematic evaluation of different flight profiles to minimize disturbance while maintaining effective monitoring capabilities.

Key Achievements

  • Designed and executed systematic flight test procedures for acoustic data collection
  • Developed Python data processing pipeline for acoustic analysis
  • Generated frequency domain visualizations showing noise characteristics at different altitudes
  • Identified optimal flight parameters to minimize wildlife disturbance
  • Contributed technical expertise to interdisciplinary research team
  • Presented findings at Bass Connections research symposium

Future Work

Future analysis will focus on correlating noise levels with observed elephant behavior responses, developing predictive models for noise impact, and refining flight protocols for field deployment in Zambia.
