Pinball Machine

Mechanical Design CAD Manufacturing

October 2025

View Repository on GitHub

Context

As part of ME421 at Duke University, I collaborated with a team to design and build "Ball Durham", a basketball-themed pinball machine for the State Fair project. The machine features multiple integrated mechanisms including spring-powered launch, flipper controls, defender mechanisms, hoop target, and ramp return system, all built into a 3×5 foot playfield at a 7° angle.

Design & Approach

The pinball machine combines traditional pinball mechanics with a basketball theme, featuring Duke and NC State branded pins, a basketball hoop target, and competitive two-player gameplay. The design emphasizes durability for public use at the state fair, intuitive controls, and engaging gameplay mechanics. The main playfield is constructed from plywood and set at a 7° angle to provide natural ball return, with a hollow area underneath for integrated mechanisms.

The project required careful integration of multiple mechanical systems: launch mechanism, flipper controls, defender mechanisms, hoop target system, ramp return, and decorative pins. Each mechanism was designed, analyzed, and tested individually before final assembly and integration.

Technical Implementation

Launch Mechanism

The launch mechanism provides players with a consistent, controllable way to introduce the ball into play using a spring-powered system similar to traditional pinball machines. The system consists of a cylindrical guide channel, compression spring, conical handle, damping foam, and force-bearing attachment. The spring stores potential energy when compressed and converts it to kinetic energy to accelerate the ball along the launch lane.

Key specifications:

  • Spring constant (k): 2.7 lb/in
  • Spring free length: 3.50 in
  • Compressed length at max load: 1.269 in
  • Maximum spring force: 6.02 lb
  • Ball velocity at max compression: 8.729 in/s

The launch mechanism was designed to provide consistent ball speed while being easy to operate for users of all ages. Triangular support braces were added at the front and back of the machine to prevent rocking when pulling back the launcher.

Flipper Mechanism

The flipper is mounted to a central steel rod acting as the rotational axis, passing through the pinball board and supported by a press-fit ball bearing. The flipper is 3D printed and wrapped with a thin rubber sleeve for consistent ball contact. Under the board, the rod connects to a 3D-printed lever arm (length dh = 1.3545 in) connected to the user's handle by a steel dowel pin. A return spring attached beneath the board provides restoring torque to return the flipper to its rest position when released.

The relationship between linear travel s of the handle and angular rotation θ of the flipper is:

s(θ) = dh sin θ

The governing equation of motion accounts for spring restoring torque and user-applied force, providing responsive and intuitive flipper control.

Defender Mechanism

To add competitive and interactive gameplay, defender mechanisms were designed for both sides. Each mechanism consists of two mirrored bodies with flaps positioned 135° from the bodies, wooden dowels, a button, a PVC lever, and a bracket guiding the lever along the plane of the board. The defenders are controlled by the opposite side's player, creating strategic interaction.

When the button is pushed, the defenders rotate until stopped in an upright position by the board cutout. Due to the weight of the defender bodies, when the button is released, they fall back into the "off" position flush with the board's surface. The final design was thinned to 0.45" thickness to minimize board clearance issues and allow smooth ball rolling when not activated.

Hoop Target Mechanism

The hoop serves as a primary target for players, designed as a basketball hoop with mounting components. The initial design included a flag mechanism that would trigger when a ball fell into the hoop, using torsional springs to release a flag indicating victory. However, during final integration, time constraints led to installing the hoop and mount components without the flag pop-up system to focus on core gameplay functionality.

The ramp design was modified during testing by increasing both width and length to make it easier for players to get the ball into the hoop, improving gameplay experience.

Ramp Return Mechanism

The ramp return system allows players to reuse their ball if they make it in the basket or miss the flippers. The ramp height matches the 7° angle of the board, ensuring that when the ball drops, it follows the same path as when missing the flippers. The ramp guides the ball back to the front of the machine, allowing quick reset for the next round.

Board Design and Pins

The main playfield is a 3×5 foot plywood board set at a 7° angle, fastened to a wooden exterior frame. The board features decorative pins designed to direct the ball toward the flippers and guide it back to the hoop. The pins reflect the basketball theme with Duke and NC State designs, each 3D printed using standard PLA material. The pins create strategic ball paths and add visual appeal to the machine.

Results & Outcomes

After fully assembling the pinball machine, comprehensive testing was conducted to ensure rigidity of mechanisms and intuitive gameplay. Testing focused on durability by repetitively playing to simulate use by children at the state fair, as well as usability improvements based on performance.

Key improvements made during testing:

  • Stability Enhancement: Added triangular support braces at front and back to prevent rocking when pulling back the launcher
  • Ball Containment: Installed wooden guide pieces along the bottom sides to prevent balls from falling below and funnel them toward the retrieval area
  • Ramp Optimization: Increased ramp width and length to make it easier to get the ball into the hoop

By the end of testing, all mechanisms showed reliable performance, making the machine ready for public use at the state fair. The machine successfully demonstrated integration of multiple mechanical systems into a cohesive, engaging gameplay experience.

Challenges & Solutions

Structural Stability

Challenge: The launch mechanism created significant forces on the machine when users pulled back the spring plunger, causing the structure to rock and shift.

Solution: Added triangular support braces cut at 45 degrees at the front and back of the machine. These braces improved stability and prevented the structure from rocking during launcher operation.

Ball Containment

Challenge: During testing, balls could occasionally escape from the bottom sides of the machine, disrupting gameplay.

Solution: Installed wooden guide pieces along the bottom sides beside the ball catcher area to prevent balls from falling below while also funneling them toward the retrieval area.

Hoop Accessibility

Challenge: Initial ramp design made it very difficult for players to get the ball into the hoop, reducing gameplay enjoyment.

Solution: Modified the ramp by increasing both its width and length, making it easier for players to successfully get the ball into the hoop and improving overall gameplay experience.

Defender Mechanism Integration

Challenge: Board clearance issues and mechanism interference when integrating defender mechanisms controlled by opposite-side players.

Solution: Thinned defender design to 0.45" thickness and added PVC elbows to levers to avoid contact with mechanisms. Added washers to constrain horizontal motion and used proper tolerances (0.015") for smooth rotation.
